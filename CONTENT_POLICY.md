# Content Policy

This repository is designed to be public. Treat every commit as something that could be quoted on social media.

## Allowed

- Links to public source material.
- Short factual metadata: title, author, publisher, date, URL.
- Original summaries and analysis.
- Original diagrams, frameworks, and checklists.
- Brief quotations when necessary for commentary, kept short and attributed.
- Public implementation patterns that do not expose private customer or company details.

## Not Allowed

- Full transcripts from YouTube, podcasts, books, courses, or talks unless explicitly licensed for redistribution.
- Downloaded videos, audio files, or subtitle files from platforms that do not permit redistribution.
- Paywalled content copied into the repository.
- Private customer data, chats, tickets, ERP records, logs, credentials, or personal information.
- Internal strategy notes, financial models, investor lists, or confidential model-debate transcripts.
- Screenshots of slides or interfaces unless they are clearly licensed or permissioned.

## Quotation Rule

Prefer paraphrase. If quoting is necessary, keep the quote brief, include the source link, and avoid compiling many fragments into a substitute for the original work.

## Analysis Rule

Separate source summary from interpretation:

- `Summary` should explain what the source says.
- `Analysis` should explain what we think it means.
- `Applications` should be framed as proposals, not claims that the source endorses them.

## Takedown / Correction

If a source owner believes content here should be removed or corrected, open an issue with the URL and reason. The default response is to remove disputed excerpts first, then review whether the summary or link should remain.
